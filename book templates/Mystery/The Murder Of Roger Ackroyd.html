In the quaint English village of King's Abbot, retired detective Hercule Poirot finds himself embroiled in yet another perplexing case. When wealthy widower Roger Ackroyd is found murdered in his study, Poirot must navigate through a maze of secrets and suspects to uncover the truth. With a masterful blend of deception and misdirection, Christie keeps readers guessing until the very end. The novel's shocking twist, controversial at the time of its publication, solidifies its place as one of the greatest mysteries ever written. "The Murder of Roger Ackroyd" remains a timeless classic that continues to captivate readers with its intricate plot and unforgettable characters.
